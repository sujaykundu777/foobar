FOOBAR 1.0

Over the past semester, ASCII has been conducting a variety of events including but not limited to Peer-to-peer workshops on various software, technologies and programming paradigms, competitive gaming sessions, and competitions based on coding and designing among others. Preparations for FooBar, planned as the flagship event of ASCII have been on for a couple of months now, and as has been the vision of ASCII, we are keen on involving the Industry on multiple fronts.


foobar 1.0 is for us, the Computer Science and Engineering students to showcase our talents in our core fields as well as other parallels. Although open to all departments of CUFE and to the Management students on the Kengeri Campus, it will serve as a platform for the students of Computer Science across deaneries come together and explore horizons.


The theme for foobar 1.0 is ‘Wirelessly Wired’. We live today in a connected world. Everything, including humans and objects are today connected to each other either through the Internet or via local, personal and other such networks. The process, initiated with the telegraph networks of the 19th century, is quickly shifting to everything wireless, radio, Bluetooth, satellite, WLAN etc. In a world where the Internet of Things is a major potential paradigm shift, we believe ‘Wirelessly Wired’ is apt as the theme of the first ever Foobar.
